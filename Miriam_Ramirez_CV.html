Undergraduate Thesis
Comparison of Explainable AI Techniques in Software Defect Prediction Feb 2026
Universidad Veracruzana, Faculty of Statistics and Informatics  ·  github.com/miriam-rz/software-defect-prediction-xai
  • Evaluated 7 ML classifiers (Decision Tree, Logistic Regression, Naive Bayes, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, SVM, MLP) on 5 NASA PROMISE datasets (CM1, JM1, KC1, KC2, PC1) for software defect prediction — applying SMOTE inside each stratified fold to address class imbalance.
  • Applied and compared 3 post-hoc XAI techniques — LIME, SHAP, and BreakDown (dalex) — to explain black-box model predictions at the instance level.
  • Proposed a novel evaluation framework for explanation quality measuring fidelity, stability, complexity, and interpretability across all model-explainer combinations — going beyond standard accuracy metrics.
